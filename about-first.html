What is FIRST?

FIRST is a global organization founded in 1989 by inventor Dean Kamen. FIRST is an acronym for "For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology" and is a non-profit organization dedicated to introducing kids of all ages to technology and engineering. FIRST hosts four global robotics leagues divided by age level, with a projected participation of over 300,000 kids in 2013. Code Orange participates in the highest level, known as the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC).

What is the FIRST Robotics Competition?

In FRC, teams of high school students are led by professional engineers to build working robots. At the beginning of every year, FIRST releases a new challenge to all FRC teams worldwide. Teams then have only six weeks to design, prototype, construct and test their unique robots. Teams then attend competitions, in which their work is tested against that of other teams. From there, teams can progress to region championships and the world championship.
